Big Data Information Extraction: Generating Seeded Named Entity Dictionary



Project Rationale and Objective

VerticalScope, Inc. owns and operates one of the most highly visited networks of online forums. These online discussion forums attract thousands of active discussions every day regarding products and services from a variety of businesses. Therefore, VerticalScope is in an unique position to glean insights from the vast amount of rich unstructured information generated by its forum users.

A core business initiative of VerticalScope is to identify names of products in these divergent verticals. Currently, the Data Science team at VerticalScope has supervised models for identifying automotive and powersports brands that is built from time-consuming and expensive manual human-labelled training sets. However, VerticalScope deals with many verticals which comes with different size, language, and content. Thus fitting a supervised model for each product/brand to each vertical can be very expensive.

Three major challenges faced by VerticalScope when identifying products are:
  • the high cost of generating training data for each type of product
  • covering large divergent types of products from all verticals, and
  • disambiguating different types of entities based on context
Therefore, in the absence or lack of training data, alternative solution is semi-supervised learning algorithms such as:
  • seeding dictionary
  • pattern-based learning
  • pattern-bootstrapping with dictionary
The goal is to create a semi-supervised machine learning algorithm for identifying product names.


Refined Problem Definition

Given the post itself and additional information (i.e. a list of “product names” on the site, the keyword statistics/measures/metrics for that site, and the meta-info of the post, the thread/thread-title), identify the potential “entity” in each post.


Key Tasks and Project Timeline

The entire duration of the research project is from July 3rd until August 31st, 2018.
Week # Main Task Key Tasks
Weeks 1-2 Training
  • Collect and create training data by annotating posts from three verticals of 2000 sentences from each (i.e. 2000 from Outdoor Vertical, 2000 from Automotive Vertical, and 2000 from Powersports Vertical).
Weeks 3-4 Experiments Round 1
  • Establish two baselines of machine learning algorithms for the Outdoor Vertical:
    • Deep Learning Bi-LSTM-CRF
    • SVM
    • Word matching with tolerence
Weeks 5-6 Experiments Round 2
  • Propose different solutions to the problem (i.e. supervised or bootstrapping) while trying to beat the baseline.
Weeks 7-8 Final Model Evaluation and Refinement
  • Measure the performance of each model and finalize models for all verticals.
  • Refine models for each vertical and all entity types.
Week 9 Technology Transfer
  • Final wrap-up

Responsibilities

  • Understand named entity recognition problem
  • Learn machine learning concepts and models; pattern-bootstrapping methodology for extracting dictionary and patterns
  • Train and create machine learning models
  • Create new pattern-dictionary method for extracting Entity names and Entity types


Expected Outcomes

  • A clean set of named entity seeds from three different Verticals (Outdoor, Automotive non-vehicle, Powersports non-vehicle) and a set of patterns for identifying named entities from each of the three Verticals
  • A finalized supervised and unsupervised machine learning model for each vertical for detecting mentions
  • Patterns for each Entity Type and general overall Entity Recognition (i.e. Mention Detection and Identification)


Summary and Outputs

Accomplishments
Although the main goal was not met, our research team still had the following accomplishments over the duration of the two-month research project.
  • Annotation: Annotated over 1000+ post sentences from over 10 verticals
  • Algorithm: Wrote scripts to detect named entities
  • Experiments and Results: Measured algorithm's performance via the F1-score
  • Deep Learning: Gained more knowledge on Neural Nets and Word Embedding
Unfortunately, our team was unsuccessful in creating a semi-supervised machine learning algorithm to detect named entities. We have instead continued with our annotations, constructed seeded dictionaries, wrote a brute-force matching algorithm and measured its performance using an F1-score statistic as our main tasks that we focused on throughout the project. We also took a look at Deep Learning to further our knowledge, particularly highlighting our focus on Neural Networks and Word Embeddings.
