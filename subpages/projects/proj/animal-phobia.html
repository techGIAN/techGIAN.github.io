Animal Phobia

MY UPs & DOWNs   |   Date Posted: September 12, 2020

Photo by Sveta Golovina on Unsplash.com

Hello! Ok well today, I decided to write something short and sweet as I am readlly swamped with work and yet would like to squeeze in a short post as I am taking a “break”. I’d like to talk about my animal phobia. Ok well, it all started with me when I was very young, say around 6? I was at my cousin’s house and they have a white samoyed dog. It was cute and fluffy (well pretty sure you know what it looks like). Its name was a Snowball if I’m not mistaken. I was petting it, or perhaps I was “trick petting” it. It’s basically when you are about to pet the dog then you just don’t. There’s a lot of “Don’t Pet Your Dog Challenges” out there (go check Instagram and Tiktok). Anyways, I think I may have done that and Snowball I think got triggered (or perhaps just thought that I was playing) and just bit me by the hand. It was really painful and was bleeding. Good thing it was already vaccinated with Anti-Rabies. But that really terrified me to the point that I got a dog phobia, i.e. scared of dogs (or near dogs). Pretty it’s called cynaphobia. And later on, generalization came with this phobia. That’s when a particular phobia developed to an even bigger phobia, in this case, zoophobia, for animals in general (I learned about the generalization of fears in my general psychology class). So for any other animals like cats, fish, birds, (of course also the wild animals), etc., I have developed a rational fear for them. However, despite the fact that I still have this fear as i am growing older, I am making some self-effort to overcome this fear. Later on, I am able to touch and pet a cat, and even have it purr on my lap! It was a big achievement for me. I am also able to pet some dogs now. Of course the fear is still there and I am not easily able to cope easily just yet (perhaps with pets maybe then things will change?). But I’m happy about the progress I have made and I do hope things will become better in the future.
