Back Pains and Posture Problems

MY UPs & DOWNs   |   Date Posted: October 12, 2020

Photo by Jesper Aggergaard on Unsplash.com

(Just an update post actually, it's been almost a month since I posted - due to many works and deadlines. But I'm well, hopfeully you are too, but today I'll talk about my back pains and posture problem; I'll keep it short. I did post the other day about Conspiracy Theories (Part 2), go check that out!)

I’d like to share what I’ve been doing currently as I seem to be getting lower back pains after sitting for way too long in my computer chair, as I have so much studying and work for long periods of time, since all classes and work becomes “Work from Home”.

Firstly, I went to my massage therapist to somehow get my lower back massaged to ease the pain. I did it twice but the pain didn’t go away. I can’t keep on coming back due to cost being expensive, and my insurance covering it has a limit. Secondly, I took some Tylenol, which are the pain reliever pills. But then again, it’s only temporary and I don’t wish to keep on taking such medicines for the rest of my life or my kidney and liver suffers. Thirdly, I went to see my family doctor hoping I could be recommended with a specialist, but she only gave me a prescription to get a lower back support (lumbar support). And so I went to this store called Stakman’s where I was able to get this item (they have medical supplies and the item that I needed turned out to be pricy, and so I got my insurance to cover the costs but only half of it sadly). So now I’m using this lumbar support which helps my back (and my stomach too!). Fourth, I bought a laptop stand for my computer, which helps me a lot with posture so that I’m sitting straight with the lumbar support and I am looking straight on my computer - and to make and help things better, I bought a magic keyboard and a magic trackpad that helps me whenever I’m working (so that I don’t have to rely on my computer when it is on the stand). It is also compatible with my iPad and hence this is a win-win situation.

It is hard while working from home, but hopefully soon, this will no longer be a problem for all of us and we go back to our old style of working. I will keep it short today and end there as I have more work to be done.
