But now that I think about it, curiosity can also be seen as a philosophy that is innate on man after all, but not as a curse. Note that before I begin each CT article, I would summarize what I have talked about previously, and then in the end summarize what was talked about.

So today, I’ll be talking about three things. One is about money, another is about the Bermuda Traingle, and the other being about the poison around us. Let’s start with money. Think about the last time you spent your money on something, whatever it is. Did you ever think about its worth? Did you ever think it was worthy? Does it even have value? Well, my friend. That’s the theory. Money doesn’t have value at all, which I guess is a paradox itself. You need money to buy things that you want and need. But having money and not spending it (but you gotta save it right?) does not make it valuable. Even if you have millions of dollars but you’re not spending them, and leaving them to rot, then they have no value. Money originated long time ago and the first form of currency was in the form of goods, i.e. trading. In order to get something that you want (or need), you must possess something of “equivalent value” and then trade it, hoping it will be a successful trade. The problem here is: (1) there is no notion of equivalency (i.e. how could one sack of rice equal 10 cobs of corn for example?), (2) who decides legitimate equivalency (well I guess amongst themselves since there wasn’t any notion of law back then?), (3) one is unable to judge whether he is at the upper hand or at the disadvantage (again, unfariness), (4) you need to have something to get something, but how do you acquire that something if you originally begin with nothing? (5) both parties have to agree (i.e if only one person wants the trade and the other doesn’t, it’s not gonna work out - similar to one-sided love). Ok, so I guess this is the motivation for how and why (or where) our currency came from. But this goes back to the question, “What is the value of money?” How much is a $1 bill worth? Well, depends on currency I suppose. So there is some intrinsic value I guess? But we only see it when we acquire things that we need and want using the money. It doesn’t have actual value if it’s not spend. So in order to see this actual value, you have to spend a lot of your money, but doing so affects your budget and earnings, which does not show good value at all. With this paradox in mind, is money that valuable after all? Remember, money can’t buy you happiness (true happiness) that is.

Now I’d like to talk about the Bermuda Triangle. Click here if you wish to learn mnore. Anyways, this specific region is located between Bermuda (north), Florida (west), and Puerto Rico (south). And there have been many stories being told about many ships and planes disappearing without trace when they enter this zone. In fact, around 4 planes and 20 boats each year have gone missing when they get caught up in the dangerous waters. Both scientists and conspiracy theories alike have thought of theories on how this phenomenon can be explained. There were theories on dangerous water waves called rouge waves, explosive underwater volcanoes, methane bubbles underneath the crust of the seafloor, magnetic compass malfunctions, and even aliens that abduct the ships and planes (including the humans) for experiment (and the triangle is simply a portal or a gateway towards the alien realm). It is also theorized that the Lost City of Atlantis could possibly exist underneath the waters of the Bermuda Triangle (I would write a whole theory about Atlantis some other time). Some do not believe any of these and just left it at “probably just a coincidence”. I myself believe that it could be a combination of these things. Perhaps there were dangerous storms that destroy the ships or planes that get sucked in by the Bermuda, and that the inhabitants of Atlantis abducts these people on board. Any debris of the destroyed ships or planes get cleaned by the underwater healing effects (or perhaps the power of rthe kings and queens of Atlantis). The abducted humans have then been forced to be citizens of Atlantis while being manipulated, controlled and brainwashed for them to never return to the surface and to keep the secrets of Atlantis safe. Of course, this sounds hysterical but nothing is a possiblity, correct? But what if the secrets of the Bermuda is protected by a secret global government organization that attempts to create mystery into the minds of people? And here we ago again with the curse of curiosity ya-di-ya-da…
