CHAPTER 01: The Root

It was a beautiful Saturday morning, the fourth of June. Though, it was a fine day, everything in that day turned out gloomy. You want to know why? Well, as for myself, I let my readers decipher what was going on. I don’t just narrate right away. Sometimes, the best way to narrate a story is by implying the main idea. This way, readers would become eager of what they’re reading.

If you were not able to decipher these events after reading the story, then it proves that you did not understand a single word you have read. Though the real meaning of the statements are hidden, but through context clues, it would be easy to derive the events!

Now, are you ready? Sit back, grab a cup of some hot beverage, and get ready for action!

As I was saying, the fourth day of the sixth month of the year was a beautiful, but gloomy Saturday morning. I came to wake up on a not-so much important day in my cousin’s house. I had to wake up early on that day. I have a dentist appointment at 10 AM. It was already quarter to 9, and yet, I haven’t prepared my most important meal of the day, breakfast.

Quickly, I rushed downstairs, then off to the kitchen, but to my surprise, sausages and scrambled eggs are prepared on the dining table. I had the thought of someone nice would already have prepared our morning meal. I was glad. I checked the kitchen, there goes my aunt, cooking more sausages.

“Let me help you cook sausages, aunt!” I exclaimed to her.

“Now, dear. I’ll do it myself,” a kind voice came out of her lips. “In the meantime, kindly toast some bread. It’s on the dining table,” she added.

I couldn’t say no, since I’m staying in their house at the meantime. I wanted to help them, since their household helper went on a 3-month vacation (woah, that’s too long!).

But before I reached for the bread, I checked the Grandfather clock, just situated beside the dining table. It was already 9. I had to prepare the toast quickly. I placed the bread inside the toaster immediately.

While the bread is being toasted, I ran to my cousin’s room. I woke her up. She was going to the dentist too. So I had to wake her up and she sat down by the dining table at once.

Aunt laid the sausages in front of her, while I got the toasts. I spread butter on the toasts and laid them on the table. The three of us ate immediately.

After eating, I took my regular bath and brushed my teeth. I was ready to go. My cousin didn’t, ‘cause we were going to be late. My aunt already had her shower before running to the kitchen. All, but one was complete in the recipe. Someone, who could, or maybe, who will, become the star of the show with what you’re going to read. This, is the beginning, the beginning of the root.

You will soon find out who this guy is. You will meet him, which actually marks the root of the true isolation. Don’t believe me? Well, I rather will.

Don’t know how he becomes the star and not me? Don’t know how he enters the picture?

Like I said, there are no revelations. All these are implied. Just reading one statement (or maybe just the title itself) may already tell you what was going on.

You have questions? Save it for later. It’s best if you keep them first. It would be logical to do, since your questions will be answered later.

How about my aunt and my cousin? How come I don’t reveal their names? I’ll reveal them soon, don’t worry.

To tell the truth, I didn’t use the words of the last sentences of the 11th paragraph up to the 16th (or maybe 17th paragraph). Writing this story made me do it.

Fiction? Some parts are fictional, and never really happened. Using hyperbolic and ironic figures of speech help me make a good story up.

Figured it out yet? Don’t worry, you’ll figure it out soon.

Well, hey… Do you want to know what happened next? Okay, then we shall. Now, turn to the next page, and watch how the events turn around. For the root of the isolation, we’ll know if he turns out to be the protagonist, or the antagonist.

CHAPTER 02: The Revelation

Woah! The chapter name! If on the previous chapter, I keep saying, “Events are implied,” here I shall reveal some of the most vital parts of the story which will then be used towards the story ending.

Take note that in revealing some names, I used code names, which may help in hiding the person’s identities.

To start off, my cousin. Her name, Annie, my Cousin Annie. She’s a good artist, who loves to draw, and read books. She also likes anime and likes to be with friends.

I have been really close with Annie, since when we were kids. I often tease her about in some things, but in the same way, we still treated each other like good friends, like good cousins, and even siblings!

Next, is my Aunt, Aunt Diet. Aunt Diet is the mother of Annie. my dad and she are siblings, which also makes Annie and I cousins.

Aunt Diet has been so nice to me and to my dad. She considers us as part of the family, and same goes to them. She is a hard worker, and also the reason why I liked being at their home.

We often help Aunt Diet and Annie with chores like washing the dishes, preparing meals, doing the laundry, cleaning the house, and so much more. Working with them is such a pleasure. And we thought of helping them in return of them accepting us into their home.

You might also ask about my dad? Hmmm… Let me think… Nope! Not yet! It would be too early for you to know. But I’ll describe him a little bit.

He’s a hard worker. He helps me in my studies when I was young, and so much more! He’s a master chef and oh man, he makes delicious dishes like banana cakes, pizzas, and more! Not too much detail about him, though. He’s not the object here. But he may become the new object later. We’ll see.

Now, let me describe the root! The root is actually one of my other cousin. He’s Annie’s big brother, and BIG BOTHER.

From the sounds of it, I may have described the root to be a BIG BOTHER, since he is the one to bother me, which leads to the isolation.

He’s name is Sammy. Sammy would be the root of all this, the root of which problems like these, arose.

Sammy has been a naughty guy. But sometimes, he could be a big help around the house. His mischief could give some smiles to the members of the house. But sometimes, this mischief can give frowns and angers. Too much of this mischief is not good as well. Remember, too much of anything is not good!

Sammy is also taking a culinary subject. He wanted to be a master chef, just like my dad!

Well, I guess I already have answered why Sammy is a big bother on a very beautiful, but gloomy June 4.

How about the root? How could Sammy be a cause? How could he be a root of isolation?

It’s too soon to speak of it. I guess, that it would be alright to reveal each one’s personalities. But what are other things you must know to complete the first phase of the story? These things are the keys to solving the mystery.

Though I may not be preceeding to our main story, it would be good to know if I give you some details, on some essential elements you need to know.

First, where is my dad? He has not been introduced in Chapter 1, right? He was only first heard of in this chapter, when I was talking about Aunt Diet. Where is he?

Second, are we even ready to go to the dentist? And who’s coming with us? My dad was supposed to be cooking, since he’s a master chef! How about Sammy? Is he going with us to the dentist? Does he have an appointment with the dentist too?

Third, how about the real story? Did you just think I wasted 3 pages for this chapter, without any real life story? And hell, ya! This is a narrator’s job.

And fourth, how come the author forgot to describe himself? Forgot to reveal himself? He’s one of the characters too, right? Is he an antagonist, or the hero?

Well, the fourth revelation got a little bit off guard. Well, yeah, he’s one of the characters. The author is not the protagonist nor the antagonist. But, he will be revealing himself soon.

Breathe in slowly, then breathe it out again slowly. Easy and come down. These questions are quite intriguing, which is actually the key to understanding the beginnings of the story.

Get ready now. Though, the third chapter is not a the main story again, at least it will now serve as the KEY in solving such an ambiguous beginning.

CHAPTER 03: The Key

The Key. What key? What am I talking about? This I actually the key for you to understand how everything began.

If you are asking where my dad is, simple and yet a long story. I shall begin with this very long story.

I had a summer Math training last April, which lasted for 1 month until May. I stayed in Annie’s house for that 1 month duration, since the training venue is near Annie’s house, and very far from where I lived (yet to be revealed later on). After training, my dad and I decided to stay after my training to help them with chores and other stuff, since their household helper went on the vacation I was telling you about last Chapter 1. It all boiled out to a life-making decision last May 30, which is actually this week, a Monday. I asked him if we could already go home by Friday, June 3. His refused, since I still have to go with Annie’s family to the Baptism of our youngest family member. I had to tell this to mom (which is not with me at the moment). I asked her if she could plead my dad to go home by that Friday. When my dad found this out, he got mad at me. But since, I had no more choice, I had to stay until Sunday, which is the actual Baptismal date. And also, he had to go back home on a Friday, to take care of some important things. I had nothing to do to stop him.

You might wonder why I needed to go home? I have classes by next week, June 13, and I really needed to go home and prepare for my upcoming classes.

Monday’s decision was life-making. It was crucial. It determines the events on June 4, which you will later know.

Going back, I didn’t know why he wanted me to stay. Ii didn’t even want to go to this Baptismal date. My classes are more important. That’s my numero uno priority, but looks like I had no choice but to abide by him.

That Friday, I felt like I needed to take a nap. On this nap of mine, I dreamt that there is something bad that could happen, some bad effect within this 3-day weekend, something bad in Annie’s house.

But since I could not recall after I woke up (5 minutes after waking up 50% of your dream will be forgotten, and 10 minutes after waking up, 90% of your dream will be forgotten), I had no reason to show my dad, and too bad, I have recalled nothing. I’m sunk!

Then he left. I slept in with Sammy on the night of June 3. Annie slept in her room that night.

And the next morning, which was June 4, were actually the events from Chapter 1.

Second answer to the question back in Chapter 2, Aunt Diet and Sammy joined Annie and I to the dentist. They dropped us in front of the clinic and the two went to the hospital. There were 2 missions we had to undergo. The first mission was to visit the dentist and the second was for the reader to figure out. (I told you that there’s just no revelation, but also analysis!)

There’s actually no waste in 3 pages there. Those pages are actually keys and revelations, which help both the author and the reader.

These pages help the author because he needed to give the readers clues to the main events, and not just giving away.

And at the same time, these keys and revelations actually function as clues for the readers to analyze.

Combining these keys and revelations together with the first events from the first chapter can cook us up conspicuous ideas. That would be the perfect recipe to create such an unvague flavor.

CHAPTER 04: The Results

As I was saying from Chapter 1, after Aunt Diet, Annie, and I ate, I took a shower and was ready to go. My cousin did not bother to take a shower. Aunt already had hers. Sammy ate breakfast while I was taking my shower. He did not bother to take a bath either.

We all head out to their van, to take care of our 2 missions, which were to go to the dentist and to undergo a special mission.

Aunt Diet drove the van, while Annie and I sat together at the front row, with Aunt Diet. Sammy lied down at the 3rd row of the van.

“Remember, we must never close the windows of the vehicle,” Aunt Diet exclaimed. “I know it’s hot, but it’s for the sake of Sammy, and for the three of us,” she added.

I heard Sammy’s mutterings from the back of the vehicle, hoping he would stop all his complaints. But he would not. I had nothing to do. I can’t stop him. I’m riding inside their van. I must show them respect.

On our drive, I became worried of Sammy, though he shut his mouth up. Aunt felt the same way as well, even before we rode the van. Annie did not feel anxious. You don’t know how the two siblings fight with each other. It was such a sibling rivalry between the two. This rivalry also provided a very good relationship between them, which made the two strong.

Sammy is not in a very good shape right now; that is why I became worried. But, he’s also a member of my Revenge List, if you know what I mean. But in his case right now, I cannot formulate a plan to seek vengeance. I did not even want to lay a finger on him in this instance.

We had to hurry. It’s almost quarter to 10, and the dentist appointment is at 10 in the morning. And the van’s gas tank is almost hitting empty. Aunt Diet and I were filled with pity when we saw Sammy’s conditions.

She sped the van up. We hit the gasoline station at once. We asked for a quick service, to fill the tank up and have paid using her credit card. The service was quick. It was less than 5 minutes. It was the quickest service in the gas station I had had.

But now is not the time to be impressed. The gas station was not our goal. It was the 2 missions. We geared up our engine and began our drive towards the quests. The engine of the van sounded as if it was a race car. “Zoom!” it went off!

We immediately went to our first mission – the dentist. Aunt Diet dropped Annie and I off in front of the clinic. She gave Annie twelve dollars and told us to give her a call once both of us were done. Then Aunt Diet drove off to prepare for the 2nd mission.

I wanted to help her for the second quest, but she said it was too dangerous. So I let her fulfill the second mission.

Annie and I walked towards the clinic. I knocked on the door, but no one answered.

“Why not try ringing the doorbell?” Annie suggested.

I gave the doorbell a loud buzz. The loud sound pierced my ears. It was really loud! But still, no one answered the door.

I checked the door. It had a little note in it, which said:

“The dentist is out. I just had a little snack. I’ll be back at exactly 10:00 AM.”

Looks like Annie and I had nothing to do but wait. Annie got her phone from her bag to check if there was anyone who sent her a message. There was none. She kept her phone, and then brought out her iPod and her earphones. Plugged one in her left ear, and the other in her right ear, and then she inserted the end to her iPod. She selected a very soft and slow song and listened to it while waiting for the dentist.

I also got my iPod, and instead of listening to music, I played some games, to entertain me. I was just too tired to listen to my music that I tried to play some fine games.

It was already 5 to 9, and yet the dentist hasn’t come by. We were both getting bored.

“How long will we wait around here?” Annie complained.

“Don’t you worry, Annie. He said he will be back at exactly 10,” I exclaimed.

My iPod is already low in battery. I have to charge it at once. But, instead, I just turned it off.

Annie also got tired listening to her music. She stowed her iPod away and just waited for the dentist to come along.

Suddenly, I saw a shadow of a man pass by. It was Dr. Clay, the dentist. Both of us said hello as he removed the note on his door. He got the key to his clinic and opened the door. We sat by the couch and waited who will be first in line in his chair. It was me.

I did not fear the dentist a single bit. I liked getting my teeth clean, and of course to have my retainers checked.

Annie also went to see Dr. Clay to have her retainers checked but she once feared him when he attaches her braces. It was hysterical moment where I laughed at her back then.

I sat down by his dental chair and I opened my teeth widely. He said that I have to keep my retainers on 24-7, since my teeth are not yet as strong as it should be.

There was really nothing to do but check. The check-up was A-OK and I rose up from his chair and sat beside Annie as Dr. Clay asked her to sit down by the dental chair.

Same thing happened with Annie, nothing really happened. We were advised to keep our retainers on.

We were ready to go, but Annie had a question to raise from Dr. Clay.

“How much is your Cleaning Services?” Annie asked, but was not that curious.

“I’ll charge you for somewhere between 5 to 6 dollars, depending on your teeth,” Dr. Clay told her.

Not as much eager, she said, “Thanks, I’ll think about it. We have to go.”

Both of us bid him goodbye as we exited the door.

I called up Aunt Diet to tell her that the examination has been complete. But no one is picking up the phone. I tried for 5 missed calls, and yet, no one is picking it up.

“I hope Aunt Diet and Sammy is alright with their missions. I’m becoming anxious,” Annie confesses her feelings.

I tried to comfort her. I had to know what was going on. I had to know if the second mission was a success. I had to know what’s our next move. I had to know the complete details as soon as possible.

We, Annie and I, still continue to worry about Aunt Diet. No text-back. No answer from the phone either. There was no hesitation. We felt anxious, grieved. Well, actually, we only stayed in front of the clinic. Dr. Clay was preparing for his next patient. We did not want to disturb him in his work. Our appointment is over and it is only polite to leave him alone in his work. But Annie and I had nowhere to go. We had to stay there.

It was ten minutes past the time when we were dismissed and still no response from Aunt Diet. We already made a total of seven missed calls and sent her four text messages. Aunt Diet did not respond to any of form of these communications.

Annie sat down by the door hedges while I continue to stand by her.

“Look at the weather, Annie. It’s about to drizzle,” I told Annie.

“Yes, I know that Carlo,” Annie grieved. “The weather is gloomy as well,” she added.

Well, yeah, my name is Carlo. Sorry, I didn’t want to know my name, but yes, she spoiled it up.

Both of us also felt the feeling of being bored. So, Annie got her iPod and listened to her favourite tunes, while I kept my eyes on my phone, if in case Aunt Diet responds.

“Oh by the way, what was the twelve dollars Aunt Diet gave to you before for?” I asked her curiously.

“You know what, I forgot to ask her. Maybe lunch money?” Annie said unsure with her answer as she shrugged her shoulders.

Fifteen minutes pasted away. We were both getting more bored and more worried. Suddenly, we heard the sound of a soft bell. Both of us were alerted. The sound came from the other side of the street. There, we saw a cat with a bell tied around its neck.

“Oh, so that’s where the sound of the bell came from,” I concluded.

“Looks like it has an owner,” Annie added.

“Huh?!” I showed her a grinned face.

“Well, you know. The cat has a bell tied around its neck. Who could have placed it there? It must have an owner,” Annie explained. “It also works like a dog tag,” she added.

The cat ran away. It must have seen us and have been startled. Too much to the excitement, Aunt Diet responded to my text message saying:

“Why have you not answered my calls? I sent 3 calls to Annie but she was not picking it up. And please don’t send me up to 7 missed calls again. My phone’s battery reduces when you do this. Thanks!”

Looks like too much of our cat excitement, we forgot to keep an eye on our phones. But we did not regret it. Our phones were set on a silent mode. But we did not anymore care. Annie checked her phone at once and found 3 missed calls from Aunt Diet. Immediately, she called her. The phone was ringing.

Aunt Diet picked it up at once and said:

“Annie, I have no more time to have a greet of ‘Hi’ and ‘Hello’ anymore. My next instruction to you is to go with Carlo and walk to the nearest restaurant, which is just in front of a hospital. Use the money I gave you to eat your lunch there. Sorry about it being an early lunch. I’ll call back later for further instructions. Bye!”

“What? What did Aunt Diet said?”

“We were told to walk to the nearest restaurant from here, which is just in front of a hospital.”

“And eat an early lunch as we wait for her next instructions.”

“You forgot to say ‘Hi’.”

“She said she had no more time to say these jibberish.”

“And Aunt Diet? How was she?”

Along the way, many curious questions still aroused my mind of what things are happening. My mind is also thinking of such a logical explanation which may give answers to these questions.

CHAPTER 07: Pity and Sympathy

The outside of the restaurant is not that bad at all. I would not care about the atmosphere. I would rather enjoy the food. If I will talk about the atmosphere outside, many homeless children would hang around the mirrors of the place. I did not care about them when I entered the place. I just didn’t wish to be dirty, that’s all. In front of the place was a hospital. It had a modern architecture, just like the “Burgies and Burgees”. Many people enter and exit the hospital. I didn’t care about the name, though. I just wanted to eat. Beside the restaurant are small stalls were people usually pass by to obtain some information on where to go. They rarely buy there. The items in stock were a little less than limited. But the main entrance of the restaurant was absolutely fantastic! It caught our attention right away. From the looks of it, food will be good here as well.

Annie and I entered at once and we asked the waiter to give us a table for two. We were sitted down on a small table just near the entrance of the restaurant. The waiter gave us the menu. We were astounded by the many selections of dishes we can choose from. But we were in a hurry as well. Our tummies are growling and we still need to make a call to Aunt Diet after we eat. I ordered the Double Cheeseburger and a Medium-sized fries. I also ordered a can of Dr. Pepper with it and a Small cup of Sundae. Annie ordered the Classic Cheeseburger and Small fries. She ordered a large bottle of soda pop and a hot fudge as well. Once the waiter got our orders, he ran to deliver the order immediately. We said that we were in a hurry.

We waited for our meal to be served. For the meantime, Annie listened to her favourite beats on her iPod while I made a short phone call to my close childhood friend.

I dialled the number and heard that the phone was ringing. Someone picked the phone up. It was my friend, Eric.

“Hello, Eric! Long time no see!”

“Hello, Carlo! I know, it has been a while.”

“How are you?”

“I’m alright. I’m on a special project right now. I’ll call you back later, alright.”

“Sure, whatever you say.”

Those were my final words before I hung the phone up. I just didn’t want to disturb him right now. He might be on an important business. But what on the world is he doing right now? That was a question that echoed in my mind.

The food was taking a while. But I’m sure that the chef wanted to make it special. I didn’t care how long it will take, as long as it will be served and it would taste well.

Looks like Annie was enjoying her tunes right now. She used to be impatient, especially when she’s hungry and meals are taking long to serve. But in this instance, she did not even care.

I was also checking my phone for updates and recent messages. Aunt Diet may also call as well. She may even send me a text message. I wanted to be notified on the recent news.

On the restaurant was also a TV. But it was turned off. I asked the waiter if I could turn it on.

“Excuse me, waiter!”

“Yes, sir. May I help you?”

“I was wondering if I could turn the TV on.”
