Mother's Day DIY Gift Ideas: Quarantine Edition

PRO-TIPs   |   Date Posted: May 09, 2020

via GIPHY

It has been almost two months since the lockdown had begun. Stores are closed everywhere and it is quite hard to buy anything, especially when Mother’s Day is just around the corner and we all would like to think of our moms on their special day. In fact, I have came up with 5 ideas of what Mother’s Day present you could give to your mom even when stores are closed or basically whenever we are on lockdown. (P.S. Not just during the pandemic of the coronavirus but basically during times of quarantine like when a zombie apocalypse or an alien invasion happens, not saying it’ll happen but it’s best to be prepared, right? Who knows what other terrible stuff happens for the rest of 2020).

via GIPHY

1. Homemade Breakfast


Nothing beats to start the day with a big breakfast! Especially if your mom is always the one preparing your breakfast everyday, give her that break on Mom’s Day. Instead of her making breakfast, why not you be the one this time? Prepare her strips of fried bacon and sunny-side up eggs with French toast and perhaps her favourite fruit like strawberry or melon on the side. Best to serve it to her in bed when she wakes up, and don’t forget to serve with a smile!

The Food Network has some breakfast recipes you can try!

Photo by Matthew Henry on Burst (by Shopify)

2. Handmade DIY Mother's Card


Our moms appreciate a mother’s day card, especially if it is handmade. She knows it’s from the bottom of our hearts and she’ll love it for sure! She’s going to keep it and never want to lose it, because she cherishes us this much. You can always get a hard cardboard, or perhaps a folder and cut it into the shape of the card as desired. Use scissors and glue to tape in colored paper and whatever else you would like to decorate the card. Perhaps include her picture on the card too or be an artist and sketch her picture on the card. Don’t forget to write a short and sweet dedication!

Having trouble making your very own DIY card? Here are some tips, tricks and techniques that can help you get started.

Photo by Karolina Bobek on Unsplash

3. Slideshow Video


If you’re like me that likes to keep old photos, then this could be a great gift to her. Prepare a slideshow of all her photos (and also of her children), and include some messages within the slideshow. Then overlay it with an amazing music; it could be her favorite song, or anything that reminds her of her children. Surprise her with this video by uploading to social media or to Youtube and send her the link. She’ll be wowed with what she’s about to see!

In making a slideshow, I always use iMovie as it is very much reliable especially for a beginner like me. Unfortunately, iMovie only runs in iOS and MacOS operating systems. If you have Windows, I can instead recommend Adobe Premier Pro which I think is really good in terms of quality comapred to iMovie; although Premier Pro comes with a decent cost. I have also tried out Sony Vegas Pro, which is another alternative to the mentioned video editing softwares. Like Premier Pro, it comes with some price. Though if you are only needing the softwares for creating a Mother's Day video for the coming Mother's Day, then maybe you can simply download the trial version - which if I'm not mistaken doesn't expire until after a week of download.

via GIPHY

4. Photo Collage


If you are a bit short on time and you still have those old photos, you can always create a collage instead. Whether it is done physically on hard copy paper or through digital means in Photoshop for instance, she’ll sure love it. Make sure you share it with the public for her and for her social media followers and friends to see, that you are proud of her as a mom!

Adobe Photoshop is top-notch with collage-making (or anything that has to do with photo editing). However, it comes with a price. An alternative that I use is GIMP, which turns out to be free for download. The downside in GIMP is that its tools aren’t that intuitive for use and that its interface (in Mac) doesn’t look too good. But still give it a try; who knows? You might like it in the end and turns out much better than Photoshop.

Photo by Danila Hamsterman on Unsplash

5. Flowers/Bouquet


Got a garden blooming in your backyard? Or does your mom have an interest in gardening? Maybe she won’t mind you take a couple of the flowers, decorate and design it to make it a nice-looking bouquet which you can present her on her special day. Make sure you think about the type of flowers she likes best. What are her favorite flowers? Roses? Tulips? Daffodils? Lilies? She’ll love the thought of it for sure, but just make sure that she’s cool with it. You don’t want to see her mad on her special day for when her flowers from her garden have been picked on by someboday else.

Photo by Shopify Partners on Burst (by Shopify)
