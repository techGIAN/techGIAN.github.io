The Impact of AI and Human Voice on Consumer Behavior

IN MY HEAD   |   Date Posted: December 25, 2020

Photo by Alex Knight on Stocksnap.io

This is for an essay/blog submisson to Voice Talent Online, giving away scholarships, and this year's theme is "Robot vs Human - Voice and Consumer Behavior". Here is the link: https://www.voicetalentonline.com/services/voice-over.

Voice imposes a strong impact on humans, a natural effect that humans exhibit. In fact, according to a research conducted by E. Simon-Thomas and D. Keltner of the Greater Good Science Centre Lab, humans are able to detect basic emotional tone from voice. Furthermore, they have concluded that people are also good in distinguishing and characterizing specific feelings off each other (eg. fear from sadness, awe from compassion and embarrassment, etc.). This powerful ability of humans has shaped the way we behave, such as for example a mother being more attuned to her baby or child’s voice or cries. It would not be a surprise that we have distinct behaviors and mannerisms that have shaped or instilled in us by simply hearing a particular voice or cry.

Take for instance a big natural calamity like a typhoon that just struck in a frequently-stormed place such as Manila in the Philippines for example. If you are hearing the news only now, would you have been moved more when you hear it from an Artificial Intelligence (AI) machine (like from a robot for instance), or if you hear it directly from the person who have experienced the tragic event first-hand? For sure, an AI machine like Amazon Alexa for instance that brings the news does not bring forth a strong impactful story, compared to the person that shared his unfortunate struggle, despite the fact that both brings the exact same news story.

It may be possible however that emotions in artificial voice could potentially sound natural, and human-like! A new startup called WellSaid Labs has devoted its work to using voice actors and artificial intelligence technologies to create synthetic man-like voices! We all know existing smart AI assistants such as Alexa, Siri, or Google Assistant, however they are still on the “computerized” end and WellSaid Labs are continuously working to outperform such AIs.

But how do synthetic AIs impact and influence us? Technology has come along a way and that many of us has started to rely on these techs and gizmos. In fact, studies from ID R&D Research Technology Labs have found out that 27% of Americans prefer to use voice-activated login methods into their devices and accounts and nearly a third (33%) would conduct transactions using voice and chatbots. One critical aspect to this is security. How secure could transactions be? For example, is somebody listening to you when you voice out your password to the chatbot, or would it be a safer call for you to type it instead when it is not visible to anybody else?

Another point of impact would be the consumer world. Between a real person and an AI that just spoke to you, advertising a certain product, would you be more likely to purchase the product if the advertiser is a real user, or if the advertisement came from an AI? These days, advertisements from social media sites like Facebook are all catered towards your personal interests. It has been proven that such sites “listen” or “look at” your browsing history and checks to see what products you may be interested in, and using recommender system machine learning AIs would recommend them to you in the form of ads. If the item is more of a tech item, it is more likely that the AI voice system can make a good deal off of you and you would likely be able to buy the product from the AI.

Robots and AI voice do have a strong impact and play a role in purchaser and consumer of products and services. And as a consumer, one has to be responsible if we will give in to the manipulations of the AI world towards the purchasing something.
