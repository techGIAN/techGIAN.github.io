The Imaginary Bro

IN MY HEAD   |   Date Posted: June 08, 2020

Photo by Ilya Mazurkevich on StockSnap.io

I guess when we are all at a young age, we all had that imaginary friend (or friends) who we believe is always at our side wherever we go. That special friend that we talk to whenever no one’s around. May it be that you are in trouble and your actual real life friends are not present to help or be with you. Your imaginary friend is always there. However at some point in one’s life, we all came to a conclusion that that imaginary friend is just “imaginary” and non-existent. That talking to an imaginary friend would seem like talking to one’s self, which would look crazy and it’s pretty much insane. Well yeah, that’s how I felt at maybe 10? That the imaginary friend is just a nobody. He cannot help me at all, and maybe just “talking” to him is sort of an outlet to release pain and pressure.

Although at my current age at mid 20s, I don’t think I have an imaginary friend. But I talk to myself all the time, well whenever when I’m alone. I think it’s more of a coping mechanism as I am mostly all by myself, and part of it stems from the fact that I am an only child. So I guess the loneliness is being repressed by being able to talk to myself to relieve myself from the sadness that just consumes me. But I guess I also beg the question, has there always been an imaginary friend, my imaginary friend?

If there was, I don’t think I have ever given him a name (maybe some of us may have given our imaginary friends names when we were little). Whenever I have some thoughts that I just feel like saying out loud when I’m alone rather than just keeping it in my head, I would say it aloud - but still in a way that no one is able to hear me from outside the doors of where I am. I would be like, “Bro, what the heck am I supposed to do?” Or perhaps, “Bruh, I have never expected this before!” Of course, there may be lots of context-specific questions or expressions (questions which I get no response obviously). But I think it’s enough to just keep it general and I would just say “Bro”. For some reason, whenever I’m worried, Bro always keeps me calm and collected. Whenever I have doubts, Bro always helps me to figure it all out. Whenever I have a question, Bro doesn’t respond per se but it just takes me sometime for me to figure it out and always just conclude that it was Bro after all. But to be honest, I’m not sure if this Bro is even an entity, a friend? A foe? Or just me?

One theory that I have is that Bro could be God? God always works in mysterious ways and has always been with us. He has always spoken of the truth, always sets us free, always makes us feel comfortable, always brings us to the destination safely, always loves. Though I still feel that this Bro is still me. How can I activate or how can I ever think of this Bro at all? So I’m saying that Bro can’t be God. He could be my spirit animal? An angel maybe? It’s really confusing as even I couldn’t figure out. It could just be a figment of my imagination.

Mental health has always been an issue. I have never told anybody as I have always told myself that it’s better to repress my feelings of anxiety and depression rather than mention this to somebody close to my life and seek for pity. I have always had thoughts of seeing a therapist to somehow maybe help me figure this all out if I can’t do it myself. I wouldn’t want to cause worry to my folks and to anybody that is close to me and care for me. So I have just thought of keeping it to myself and probably hopefully Covid ends soon so I could see a specialist. I sometimes feel that I’m going crazy, and depressed. Writing how I feel helps a lot too. My Bro couldn’t even figure this one out either. It may be that Bro is just me all along, trying to dig deep into my inner self searching for answers but couldn’t find anything…

P.S. I hope my thoughts and story on Bro doesn’t discourage you in seeing and seeking help. I was holding back at first. But now, I have gained the courage to see somebody. I just need to wait for the pandemic to be over.
