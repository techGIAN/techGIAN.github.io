When Your Passion Isn't For You

IN MY HEAD   |   Date Posted: January 04, 2020

Photo by Matthew Henry on Burst (by Shopify)

Ever thought of something that you really really like doing, something you really love, idk maybe a hobby? Or even something more serious such as career-related? Here’s an example of what I mean. We can see that in high school, we tend to believe that whatever program we choose to do in university ends up being our actual job after you graduate from university. Welp, apparently, that doesn’t seem to happen in all cases. Ok, well there are times that you are lucky enough that you are able to pick the program that your heart desires during high school and you are able to stick with it, mind, body and soul ’til the end. But in some cases (or maybe most?), this just doesn’t seem to be the case. I’m in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics and I see a lot of my peers from my program that apparently are “disappearing” year after year, probably they stopped school and wanted to take a gap year to do their personal thing or business (which isn’t really any of my business), or probably they were in internships (a lot decided to continue working and just stayed with their jobs and dropped out of school), I also have a few friends who just decided to give up and switch programs and some too who just chose to drop. This is the example. Why did they decide to drop out from school, or do their personal business when they can do it while continue doing school? I mean, i thought that the end goal was to finish education and get a job. Or is it? But then again, I cannot and do not ever want to interfere with other people’s personal lives. Ok, so what if I just simply take this argument to my own shoes? Does dropping out of school, if in the event I do it, a sign that i am a coward - that I am backing out of a challenge?

As crazy as it seems, it may not seem to farfetched. On one end, it can be seen as an avenue of growth and yearning. You mature because you know in yourself that it is a battle that cannot be won over. You know when to quit ahead of the game because you know that there’s no point in the end and it will never be worth it. Kind of like engaging in an argument with some irrational person who would use unreasonable arguments and even arguments that would attack your person’s self, just to win in the conversational debate. You can simply reason out that you haven’t found your passion yet, you haven’t found what really loves and attracts you and that thing that you would like to hold on to for the rest of your life. Finding that passion is hard.

Ok, but on the other end - this can’t go on forever. You can’t keep on looking for that passion, moving from one dish to another as if you were trying various meals in an all-you-can-eat-buffet. It’s ok to have that minor setback, but this wastes lots of time and can cause too much unnecessary delays. So this is where the fear stems a long (at least for myself). What if the thing I am passionate about, the things I do right now, aren’t really for me? What if I am doing them and just wasting my time because in the future, this is not what I will be aligning with? It’s so scary because I feel like I shouldn’t be making mistakes (with my decisions). Although a lot of people keep on saying “I’m still too young” or they would say something like “Age doesn’t really matter when reaching your goals”, my inner personality just screams that I cannot screw up, I cannot f*ck up and that I should do it right.

To be honest, I am not too sure if I have made the right decision. Or maybe some of you feel the same? But in my personal self, this thought of “What if my passion isn’t for me?” is just so scary since it could be true. There is still some possibility of it being false. But who knows? I will still like to continue to fight my battles, not to give up, as a positive remark. But as now, I just do not know what would be an indicating sign that I have hit a deadend point, a point wherein I have to backtrack and find another path towards my goals and dreams.
